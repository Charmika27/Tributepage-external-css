Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Early Life

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, India. He came from a humble background and had a deep interest in science and aeronautics from an early age.

Achievements

Dr. Kalam played a crucial role in India's missile development programs, earning him the title "Missile Man of India." He was instrumental in the success of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. In 2002, he became the 11th President of India, serving until 2007.

Legacy

Dr. Kalam was a visionary leader and an inspiration to millions. He emphasized education, innovation, and youth empowerment. His books, especially "Wings of Fire," continue to inspire generations. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture, leaving behind an enduring legacy.
